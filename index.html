Designed to fail

final project

over time, furniture worn down, lose their function and return to be raw materials. in their "second life" they are getting a new character. the project examines the scale between 'standard' and 'improvised', and failure as an opportunity in the life of the furniture. what are the opportunities that the failure creates? does failure can bring to the world a new useful object? this is a summary of research in material failure: bending, tearing, breaking. touch of one material with another, which will fail first? How will it look? the project deals with the design of failure. it uses the potential of allowing objects to be flat in their "first life", from the factory, to the store, and up to the costumers. it deals with the choreography of failure, the destruction that gives birth to the object. complex object trapped inside surface. One bending causes a chain reaction, a new object is born out of the surface. this way the furnitures forms: stool, table and bar stool.

  • materials: aluminium, plywood, oak

  • published on:
    • designboom

Green milk

five fingers furniture

the green milk crate of Tnuva, the largest milk products company in israel, became iconic in the israeli material view. its purpose is to transfer milk bags across israel. is that “well engineering” made it resistant, so as popular among the “five fingers discount” products. you can find the “Green Crate” anywhere: in the back of the plumber pickup, in the local pub used as a stool, on the back of a bicycles as a carrier basket, hanged on a wall in the kids room as a shelves, and more. only obvious that a product which is already “owned” by half of the israeli population should go another step, to become a proper domesticated furniture.

  • materials: playwood, “green milk crate”, cotton strap belt.

  • collaborators: dan eliash.
